# 🛒 E-Commerce Sales Performance Dashboard — Indian Regions
### Period: October 2023 – September 2025

## 📋 Project Description

This Power BI dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of e-commerce sales performance across **four major Indian regions** (North, East, West, South) over a **2-year period**. It tracks revenue, profitability, order behavior, product performance, and regional trends — designed to help business stakeholders make data-driven decisions quickly.

The dashboard answers key business questions such as:
- Which month, day, and quarter drives the most revenue?
- Which region and product category leads sales?
- How do customers prefer to pay?
- What percentage of orders rely on discounts to convert?

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 1. 📅 Monthly Sales Trend
- **May** was the strongest month at **₹50.8M**, likely driven by summer demand and seasonal promotions.
- Sales show a **gradual decline** from May through February, with **February being the weakest** at ₹39.8M — suggesting a post-holiday slowdown.
- The relatively **flat mid-year performance** (June–November range of ₹42M–₹46M) indicates a lack of mid-year sales campaigns.

### 2. 🗺️ Regional Breakdown
- **North India leads** with ₹143.58M (≈27% of total revenue), followed closely by East (₹135.81M), West (₹131.05M), and South (₹123.23M).
- The gap between North and South is **₹20.35M** — suggesting the South region has untapped growth potential.
- All four regions contribute relatively equally, indicating a **well-distributed national presence**.

### 3. 🛍️ Category Performance
- **Home Décor** tops all categories at ₹57.2M, followed by Furniture (₹56.6M) and Clothing (₹55.1M).
- **Groceries** is the weakest category at ₹47.9M — possibly due to higher competition or lower basket sizes.
- The spread across categories is narrow (₹47.9M–₹57.2M), suggesting **balanced category management**.

### 4. 💳 Payment Methods
- All five payment methods (UPI, Debit Card, Credit Card, COD, Net Banking) show very close adoption rates, ranging from **₹101.95M (UPI) to ₹111.47M (Net Banking)**.
- **Net Banking leads** slightly, which is unusual — UPI typically dominates Indian e-commerce. This may reflect a higher-value, older customer segment.
- COD (Cash on Delivery) remains significant at ₹108.88M, highlighting **trust challenges** in digital payments among a portion of customers.

### 5. 🏆 Top Products
- **Headphones** is the single best-selling product at ₹0.857M — significantly ahead of all others.
- The top 15 products are closely clustered between ₹0.594M–₹0.857M, suggesting **no single dominant blockbuster product**.
- Multiple **Lamp variants** appear in the top 15 (4 entries), indicating strong demand for lighting products worth exploring as a focus category.

### 6. 📆 Day & Quarter Performance
- **Monday is the best-performing day** at ₹81.8M, followed by Wednesday (₹79.6M) — suggesting customers plan and purchase at the start of the work week.
- **Tuesday is the weakest day** (₹71.2M) — a potential target for flash sales or push campaigns.
- **Q2 (₹137M)** is the strongest quarter, with Q3 and Q4 closely behind at ₹134M and ₹136M respectively. **Q1 is the weakest at ₹127M**.

### 7. 🏷️ Discount Dependency
- **80.04% of orders include a discount** — an alarmingly high figure that raises serious concerns about margin sustainability and customer price sensitivity.
- Only **19.96% of orders are full-price**, indicating customers may be conditioned to wait for deals before purchasing.

---

## 🚨 Business Problems to Solve

### Problem 1 — Dangerous Discount Dependency
**80% of orders are discount-driven.** At a 14.94% profit margin, aggressive discounting is eroding profitability. The business needs a strategy to shift customers toward full-price purchasing through loyalty programs, value-added services, or exclusive member pricing.

### Problem 2 — South Region Underperformance
The South region generates **₹20M less than the North** despite being a large consumer market. This signals either weak brand penetration, logistics gaps, or ineffective regional marketing. A dedicated South India growth strategy is needed.

### Problem 3 — February Revenue Slump
February is consistently the weakest month (₹39.8M vs. ₹50.8M in May). There are no major sales events anchoring this period. Introducing a targeted February campaign (e.g., Valentine's Day push, end-of-financial-quarter deals) could recover ₹5–10M in lost revenue.

### Problem 4 — Low Grocery Category Performance
Groceries lag all other categories significantly. Given that grocery is a **high-frequency purchase category**, underperformance here suggests either poor assortment, pricing issues, or delivery reliability problems that need investigation.

### Problem 5 — Thin Profit Margins
A **14.94% net profit margin** on ₹533.67M in revenue is relatively low for e-commerce. Combined with high discount dependency, this signals a need to audit operational costs, supplier contracts, and last-mile delivery costs to protect bottom-line health.

### Problem 6 — Tuesday Dead Zone
Tuesday consistently delivers the lowest sales (₹71.2M). This is actionable — targeted email campaigns, flash deals, or push notifications on Tuesday mornings could help flatten the weekly revenue curve.

---

## 💡 Recommendations

1. **Launch a Discount Reduction Program** — Introduce a tiered loyalty program where repeat customers get points instead of discounts, gradually reducing discount dependency below 60%.

2. **Double Down on South India** — Increase regional marketing spend, partner with local influencers, and improve delivery SLAs in South India to close the ₹20M gap with North.

3. **Create a February Mega Sale** — Design a dedicated February campaign (Republic Day extension + Valentine's + End-of-Winter) to prevent the sharp revenue drop that currently occurs.

4. **Amplify Lamp & Lighting as a Sub-Category** — With 4 Lamp products in the top 15, create a dedicated "Lighting" storefront section with curated collections, bundles, and interior design content.

5. **Investigate UPI Underperformance** — UPI is India's dominant payment rail, yet it ranks last here. Investigate checkout friction, failed transaction rates, or UI/UX issues in the UPI payment flow.

6. **Tuesday Activation Campaigns** — Run weekly "Tuesday Deals" push notifications and email campaigns to recover the ₹8–10M gap between Monday and Tuesday performance.

7. **Headphones Category Expansion** — The top product is Headphones by a wide margin. Expand the electronics accessories range (earbuds, speakers, cases) to capitalize on this proven demand.
